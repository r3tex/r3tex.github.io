lifestyle

How I actually live when I'm not composing, coding, or running a company. Four disciplines that mostly stay out of the other panels but account for a lot of the days.

// weightlifting

Olympic weightlifting, snatch and clean & jerk. I've trained for years under Gabriel Sincraian, Olympic medalist and coach in Romania. Two lower-back surgeries behind me and still lifting.

The goal is to qualify for the World Masters Championship in the men's 65 kg, 40+ category. Qualification total is 170 kg. I'm at 72 kg snatch and 95 kg clean & jerk. Close enough to feel it, far enough to keep training hard. A few smaller competitions come first.

// vegan

Vegan since 2019. I don't think humans and animals share some inherent godly value. My ethical case is more practical: I want to live in a world with kind people, and the behavior that produces kind people includes not eating them. After that comes the discipline, the health, and the environmental math for the world I'm leaving my son.

Weightlifting at 65 kg on a plant-based diet is trivial. Protein isn't the bottleneck people assume. Legumes, beans, tofu, seitan, things baked with vital wheat gluten. The harder problem is cooking. Most people, including trained chefs, don't know how to build a plate that balances protein, carbs, fat, fiber, salt, and umami in the same bite.

Three things I think the typical reader underestimates:

  • The epidemiology. The data on animal products and long-term health outcomes is far more rigorous than the everything-in-moderation industry narrative admits.
  • The land and emissions cost. Animal husbandry has claimed more acreage, water, and CO2-equivalent than almost any other human activity. Attenborough has been crying about it for decades.
  • The violence. Even "friendly" Swedish farms send their animals to abattoirs to be mangled and gassed. The industry is brutal in a way that is literal, industrial, and hidden on purpose.

// meditation
